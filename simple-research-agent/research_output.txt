US-Iran War Reasons:

The United States went to war with Iran primarily due to several factors including:

1. **Nuclear Proliferation**: One of the main triggers has been Iran's nuclear program, which the US and its allies viewed with suspicion, fearing potential nuclear weapon development.
2. **Regional Influence**: The US aimed to counter Iran's influence in the Middle East, where it has sought to establish its presence and support groups that oppose US interests.
3. **Support for Terrorism**: The US has accused Iran of supporting terrorist organizations, which has fueled tensions and justified military action in the eyes of US policymakers.
4. **Historical Grievances**: The relationship stems from historical events, notably the 1979 Iranian Revolution that ousted the US-backed Shah and the subsequent hostage crisis, which left deep-seated animosities.
5. **Geopolitical Strategy**: The US seeks to maintain hegemony in the region and ensure the stability of allies, particularly Israel, which views Iran as a significant threat.
